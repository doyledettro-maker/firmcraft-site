## 1. Revenue-Based Financing (RBF)

Revenue-based financing lets you borrow against existing or projected revenue streams. You repay a fixed percentage of monthly revenue until you hit a repayment cap (typically 1.3x–2x the principal), then the obligation ends. No equity changes hands.

### Lighter Capital

- **What they fund:** B2B SaaS and technology companies
- **Minimum revenue:** ~$200K ARR ($15K–$20K MRR sustained for 3+ months)
- **Funding amount:** Up to 33% of annualized revenue run rate (e.g., $330K on $1M ARR); maximum facility up to $10M across multiple rounds
- **Repayment:** 2%–8% of monthly net revenue; repayment cap of 1.3x–1.5x the funded amount; typical 3-year term but can pay off early if revenue grows quickly
- **What you give up:** No equity, no board seats, no personal guarantees. They take a security interest in business assets
- **Timeline:** Weeks, not months
- **Firmcraft fit: STRONG.** Lighter Capital is one of the best fits for a services+SaaS hybrid. Their $200K ARR floor is achievable early, the repayment cap is modest (1.3x–1.5x), and they explicitly serve tech companies including those with consulting revenue. The variable repayment adjusts to slower months, which helps with consulting revenue seasonality.

### Founderpath

- **What they fund:** B2B SaaS companies exclusively (bootstrapped focus)
- **Minimum revenue:** $10K MRR (~$120K ARR) — the lowest bar in the market
- **Funding amount:** Up to 4x MRR for standard facilities; larger facilities for $3M–$5M+ ARR companies
- **Repayment:** Discount rates of 7%–13% depending on risk score; fixed monthly payments over 12–24 months (up to 36 months)
- **What you give up:** No equity, no fees, no warrants, no prepayment penalties
- **Timeline:** Can fund within days after connecting revenue/banking data
- **Firmcraft fit: MODERATE-TO-STRONG for SkillCalibrate specifically.** Founderpath is laser-focused on B2B SaaS subscription revenue. The $10K MRR minimum is very accessible. However, they won't underwrite the consulting side of the business — this works best once SkillCalibrate has its own demonstrable MRR independent of Firmcraft consulting contracts.

### Capchase

- **What they fund:** B2B SaaS companies
- **Minimum revenue:** ~$1M ARR
- **Funding amount:** Up to 70% of ARR
- **Repayment:** 5%–12% fee structure; automated repayment as revenue comes in
- **What you give up:** No equity; they take a position against receivables/revenue streams
- **Timeline:** Underwriting and offers within 48 hours
- **Firmcraft fit: LATER STAGE.** The $1M ARR minimum puts this out of reach at early-revenue stage, but it becomes a strong option once SkillCalibrate scales. Capchase is a direct lender (uses its own balance sheet), which means faster decisions and fewer intermediaries.

### Pipe

- **What they fund:** Companies with predictable recurring revenue streams
- **Minimum revenue:** Not publicly specified, but requires demonstrable recurring revenue patterns
- **Funding amount:** Based on trading limit determined by revenue analysis; you can trade any amount up to your limit
- **Repayment:** Revenue streams repay automatically as they come in; flat fee structure
- **What you give up:** No equity, no restrictive covenants
- **How it works:** Pipe operates as a marketplace — you "trade" future revenue streams for upfront capital. Institutional investors buy them. Repayment happens automatically as those subscriptions pay.
- **Firmcraft fit: MODERATE.** Works well for SaaS subscription revenue. Less useful for consulting revenue unless you have contracted retainers with predictable payment schedules. Best suited once SkillCalibrate has enough subscription volume to trade.

### Clearco

- **What they fund:** Primarily e-commerce, DTC, and subscription box businesses
- **Minimum revenue:** Varies; focused on businesses with demonstrable online revenue
- **Funding amount:** Based on revenue analysis
- **Repayment:** 5%–8% fees (5% for 4-month advances, 8% for 6-month advances)
- **Firmcraft fit: WEAK.** Clearco's sweet spot is e-commerce and DTC brands, not B2B SaaS or consulting. Other RBF providers are better aligned.

### RBF Summary for Firmcraft

**Best near-term option:** Lighter Capital (serves tech companies broadly, low minimum, no equity) or Founderpath (lowest MRR floor, bootstrapped SaaS focus). **Best growth-stage option:** Capchase once you cross $1M ARR.

## 2. Indie / Calm VC Funds

These funds are designed for companies that want to build profitable, sustainable businesses — not unicorns. They use structures that align investor returns with founder earnings rather than forcing a liquidity event.

### Calm Company Fund (formerly Earnest Capital / Calm Fund)

- **Check size:** $75K–$250K; can lead rounds of $500K–$1M
- **Structure:** Shared Earnings Agreement (SEAL) — not equity, not debt. Investors receive a percentage of "Founder Earnings" (net income + founder compensation above a threshold) on a quarterly basis
- **What you give up:** A percentage of Founder Earnings above a set threshold until a return cap is reached. No board seats, no forced exits, full operational control retained
- **Return cap:** Once investors hit their return cap through earnings distributions, the obligation ends. If you later raise venture capital or sell, the SEAL can convert to equity at pre-negotiated terms
- **Exit expectations:** None. The SEAL creates zero pressure to raise further rounds or sell. Founders can run the business indefinitely and pay back investors through profits
- **Key differentiator:** The Founder Earnings Threshold means investors don't take a cut of your early, modest salary — only earnings above a reasonable level. This is explicitly designed for bootstrapped, profitable companies
- **Firmcraft fit: EXCELLENT.** This is arguably the single best structural fit for Firmcraft's situation. The SEAL was designed for exactly this profile: profitable, founder-operated, no interest in a venture-scale exit. You keep full control, pay investors from profits as they grow, and never face pressure to sell or raise more. The fund explicitly targets bootstrapped companies.

### TinySeed

- **Check size:** $120K for the first founder + $60K per additional founder ($120K–$220K total)
- **Equity:** 10%–12% permanent equity stake
- **Structure:** Year-long remote accelerator program plus capital investment
- **What you give up:** 10%–12% equity permanently. However, no board seats, no forced exit timeline. TinySeed is designed to give founders optionality — reinvest profits, take dividends, sell eventually, or run forever
- **Program requirements:** Must be a subscription software company (SaaS), post-revenue preferred. Year-long program includes mentorship, community, and salary support during the program year
- **Exit expectations:** TinySeed expects returns but doesn't require a specific exit. They're comfortable with dividend-paying companies. Their model works when portfolio companies grow into 7- or 8-figure businesses
- **Application:** Opens September 1 for 2026 cohorts; rolling batches
- **Firmcraft fit: STRONG for SkillCalibrate, but considerations exist.** TinySeed is SaaS-focused, which aligns with SkillCalibrate. The 10%–12% equity is permanent (no return cap like the SEAL), so you're giving up ownership forever. The accelerator component adds real value (mentorship, peer community). Main question: does SkillCalibrate qualify as the primary product, or do they view Firmcraft consulting as the core business? They want SaaS companies, not consulting firms with a SaaS product on the side.

### Indie.vc

- **Status:** Indie.vc effectively wound down its active fund operations. The original fund (Bryce Roberts / O'Reilly AlphaTech Ventures) stopped making new investments. The concept influenced Calm Company Fund and others, but Indie.vc itself is no longer actively deploying capital.
- **Firmcraft fit: NOT AVAILABLE.** Look to Calm Company Fund as the spiritual successor.

### Calm Capital (distinct from Calm Company Fund)

- **Focus:** Acquiring or investing in profitable SaaS ventures with established customer bases and strong margins
- **Structure:** More of an acquisition/growth equity model than early-stage investing
- **Firmcraft fit: PREMATURE.** Calm Capital targets more mature SaaS businesses. Could be relevant later if SkillCalibrate grows into a standalone product with significant revenue.

### Indie VC Summary for Firmcraft

**Best fit:** Calm Company Fund's SEAL structure — purpose-built for profitable bootstrapped companies, no equity dilution, no exit pressure. **Runner-up:** TinySeed if you're willing to trade 10%–12% permanent equity for a strong accelerator community and the SkillCalibrate SaaS story is leading.

## 3. SBA Loans and Lines of Credit

Government-backed loans through the Small Business Administration. The SBA doesn't lend directly — it guarantees a portion of the loan, reducing risk for participating lenders. Tech consulting businesses are well-served by SBA programs.

### SBA 7(a) Loan Program

- **Maximum amount:** $5 million
- **Current rates (2026):** Variable rates based on WSJ Prime (6.75%) plus markups: Prime + 3.0% for loans over $350K (9.75% max), up to Prime + 6.5% for loans under $50K (13.25% max)
- **Guarantee fees (FY2026):** 0.25% of guaranteed portion across all loan sizes — a significant reduction from prior years
- **Repayment terms:** Up to 10 years for working capital and equipment; up to 25 years for real estate
- **Collateral:** Required for loans over $500K. Personal guarantees required from owners with 20%+ ownership
- **Credit requirements:** No SBA minimum, but lenders typically want 680–720+ personal credit score
- **Approval rate for consulting:** 72%–78% for professional services firms (law, accounting, consulting) — among the highest approval rates by industry
- **Timeline:** 30–90 days from application to funding, depending on lender
- **Use of funds:** Working capital, equipment, hiring, marketing, business acquisition — very flexible
- **Firmcraft fit: STRONG.** Professional consulting businesses have excellent SBA approval rates. The 7(a) is versatile and can fund virtually any growth need. The main trade-off is personal guarantees and collateral requirements — this is real debt, not revenue-sharing. Best used for specific, planned growth investments (hiring, marketing spend, equipment) rather than general runway.

### SBA Microloans

- **Maximum amount:** $50,000 (average loan ~$13,000)
- **Rates:** Typically 8%–13%, set by the intermediary lender (nonprofit community-based organizations)
- **Terms:** Maximum 6-year repayment
- **Collateral:** Intermediaries generally require some collateral and personal guarantee
- **Requirements:** Must be U.S. citizen or national. Distributed through nonprofit intermediary lenders, not banks directly
- **Restrictions:** Cannot be used to pay existing debts or purchase real estate
- **Timeline:** Faster than 7(a); weeks rather than months
- **Firmcraft fit: MODERATE.** Good for a small, targeted investment (equipment, initial marketing push, contractor hiring). The $50K cap limits its usefulness for significant growth capital, but the lower barrier to entry makes it accessible very early. Could be a useful first step before qualifying for larger SBA products.

### SBA Community Advantage Loans

- **Maximum amount:** $350,000
- **Who lends:** Nonprofit CDCs, CDFI institutions, Microloan Intermediaries — mission-driven lenders focused on underserved communities
- **Terms:** Up to 10 years for working capital; up to 25 years for real estate
- **Target:** Businesses in low-income areas or underserved communities
- **Firmcraft fit: SITUATIONAL.** Depends on where Firmcraft is physically located and whether it qualifies as serving an underserved community. If you're in a qualifying area of Texas, this can be a good middle ground between microloans and full 7(a) loans with potentially more favorable terms.

### Texas-Specific Programs

- **Texas Small Business Credit Initiative (TSBCI):** $472 million in federal SSBCI 2.0 funds allocated to Texas, focused on enhancing small business access to capital with emphasis on underserved businesses
- **Texas FAST Partnership Program:** Non-dilutive capital to help small businesses navigate federal SBIR grants — essentially funding to help you apply for more funding
- **Texas Emerging Technology Fund:** Supports Texas-based tech startups commercializing innovative products, including information technology

### SBA Summary for Firmcraft

**Best near-term option:** SBA Microloan for an early targeted investment. **Best growth option:** SBA 7(a) once you need $100K+ in growth capital and can demonstrate 1–2 years of financials. Consulting firms have excellent approval rates.

## 4. SBIR/STTR Federal Grants

The Small Business Innovation Research (SBIR) and Small Business Technology Transfer (STTR) programs provide non-dilutive grants for R&D. Reauthorized through September 2031 as of April 2026.

### NSF SBIR/STTR — Artificial Intelligence Topic

- **Phase I:** Up to $275K–$305K for 6–18 months of feasibility/proof-of-concept work
- **Phase II:** Up to $1,000,000 for continued R&D
- **Total potential:** Up to $2,000,000 across phases
- **Application process:** Rolling submissions via Project Pitch system (NSF reviews within 3 weeks, then invites full proposal if promising)
- **AI workforce relevance:** NSF explicitly funds educational tools, AI-ready workforce initiatives, and AI systems for the public good. SkillCalibrate's workforce training angle aligns well
- **Current status:** NSF temporarily paused new Project Pitches due to the lapse in congressional authorization (now resolved with April 2026 reauthorization), but is processing previously received pitches. New submissions should reopen soon
- **Requirements:** Must be a U.S. small business with 500 or fewer employees. Principal Investigator must be primarily employed by the company
- **Firmcraft fit: STRONG for SkillCalibrate.** The intersection of AI + workforce development is a sweet spot for NSF funding. SkillCalibrate as an AI-powered workforce training platform could make a compelling case, especially if you can frame the R&D component around novel AI approaches to skills assessment or training personalization. The rolling submission process means you don't have to wait for annual windows.

### DoD SBIR/STTR

- **Phase I:** $75K–$110K for 3 months (varies by component)
- **Phase II:** Up to $750K–$1.8M for extended R&D
- **Direct to Phase II:** Up to $1.25M (skipping Phase I if you can demonstrate prior feasibility)
- **Strategic Breakthrough Awards (new in 2026):** Up to $30 million for breakthrough innovations
- **Topic areas:** AI is a priority across DoD components. AFWERX (Air Force), DARPA, and Army all have active AI solicitations
- **Application:** Through dodsbirsttr.mil; topics published on rolling basis
- **Firmcraft fit: MODERATE.** DoD applications require framing your technology for defense use cases. If SkillCalibrate could serve military workforce training or if Firmcraft's AI operations methodology has defense applications, there's potential. But the proposal effort is significant and the defense framing may not be natural for your business.

### DOE SBIR

- **Phase I:** Similar structure to NSF ($200K–$300K range)
- **Focus areas:** Energy-related AI applications
- **FY2026 solicitations:** Active
- **Firmcraft fit: WEAK** unless SkillCalibrate has energy sector workforce training applications.

### SBIR Summary for Firmcraft

**Best fit:** NSF SBIR, specifically under the AI topic with a workforce development angle for SkillCalibrate. The rolling submission process, $275K+ Phase I awards, and explicit interest in AI workforce tools make this the most natural match. Budget 40–60 hours for a competitive proposal. Consider the Texas FAST Partnership Program (mentioned above) for grant-writing support.

## 5. Non-Dilutive Programs: Cloud Credits, Accelerators, and Competitions

### AWS Activate

- **Standard tier:** Up to $100,000 in AWS credits delivered largely upfront
- **AI/ML tier:** Up to $300,000 for generative AI startups with compute-heavy workloads
- **Beyond credits:** AWS technical support, training, architecture guidance; access to the AWS partner ecosystem for go-to-market; hiring signal (AWS expertise is in-demand)
- **Requirements:** Must be a startup (self-funded or funded); working product or prototype
- **Cost:** Free to apply; no equity taken
- **Firmcraft fit: STRONG.** If either Firmcraft or SkillCalibrate runs on AWS (or could), this is essentially free infrastructure for 1–2 years. The AI/ML tier at $300K is substantial if SkillCalibrate uses significant compute for AI model training or inference. Apply immediately — there's no reason not to.

### Google for Startups Cloud Program

- **Credits:** Up to $350,000 in Google Cloud credits for AI startups
- **Beyond credits:** Technical mentorship, networking events, go-to-market support
- **Requirements:** Startup stage (seed through Series A equivalent), building on Google Cloud
- **Firmcraft fit: STRONG** if building on GCP. Same logic as AWS Activate — free infrastructure.

### Microsoft for Startups (Founders Hub)

- **Credits (2026 update):** Bootstrapped founders without investor backing now receive up to $5,000 in Azure credits (significantly reduced from prior years). $150K requires Investor Network affiliation
- **Beyond credits:** GitHub Copilot enterprise seats, Azure OpenAI Service access (GPT-4, o1 models) with enterprise SLA
- **Firmcraft fit: MODERATE.** The credit reduction for bootstrapped founders is disappointing. However, the GitHub Copilot seats and Azure OpenAI access have real value if you're building AI features into SkillCalibrate. Worth applying even at the $5K tier for the dev tooling alone.

### NVIDIA Inception Program

- **Cost:** Free to join, no equity, no application fees, rolling admission
- **Cloud credits:** Access to partner credits including up to $100K in AWS Activate and up to $150K in Nebius AI cloud credits
- **Training:** Free NVIDIA Deep Learning Institute (DLI) training credits and SDK access
- **Hardware:** Preferred pricing on NVIDIA hardware and software
- **Networking:** Inception Capital Connect links members with NVIDIA's VC network; marketing amplification through newsletters and case studies
- **Mentorship:** Pairing with NVIDIA technical experts for AI model optimization
- **Requirements:** At least one developer, working website, officially incorporated, less than 10 years old
- **Firmcraft fit: EXCELLENT.** Zero downside — free to join, no equity, and the benefits stack with other programs (the AWS credits through Inception can be additive to direct AWS Activate credits). If SkillCalibrate uses any GPU compute or AI/ML workloads, the DLI training and technical mentorship add real value. Apply immediately.

### Notable Competitions and Grants

- **Arch Grants (St. Louis):** $100K equity-free grants for startups. Requires relocation consideration but worth investigating terms
- **MIT $100K Competition:** Cash prizes for business plans; open to non-MIT participants in some tracks
- **a2 Collective Pilot Awards:** Up to $200K in non-dilutive grants for AI + AgeTech (workforce development could potentially qualify depending on framing)

### Non-Dilutive Summary for Firmcraft

**Apply immediately (no cost, no equity):** NVIDIA Inception, AWS Activate, Google for Startups Cloud Program. **Apply soon:** Microsoft Founders Hub (limited value but still free). These programs stack — you can be in all of them simultaneously.

## 6. Profit-Sharing / Revenue-Share Agreements

These structures let an investor provide capital in exchange for a percentage of revenue or profits until a repayment cap is reached. Once the cap is hit, the obligation ends completely and you retain 100% of the company.

### Calm Company Fund SEAL (covered in Section 2)

- **Structure:** Percentage of Founder Earnings (net income + founder comp above threshold) until return cap is reached
- **Investment:** $75K–$250K
- **Cap:** Negotiated per deal; once reached, obligation ends
- **This is the gold standard for this structure.** See Section 2 for full details.

### Chisos Capital (Convertible Income Share Agreement / CISA)

- **Check size:** $15K–$50K
- **Structure:** Founders repay a percentage of salary, but only when salary exceeds $40K/year (salary floor protection)
- **Repayment cap:** Starts at 2x, but can be reduced to 1x–1.5x through early repayment or reaching a qualified financing round ($3M+)
- **Equity component:** Some equity is granted initially, but founders claw it back proportionally as they make payments. Pay back 50% of the repayment cap → reclaim 50% of the equity granted
- **What you give up:** Temporary equity stake (reclaimed through payments) + percentage of personal salary above threshold
- **Firmcraft fit: MODERATE.** The $15K–$50K check size is small — more of a bridge or very early capital injection. The salary-based repayment (not revenue-based) is an unusual structure. Best suited for pre-revenue or very early founders who need personal runway, not growth capital for a profitable business. Could work as supplemental capital alongside other sources.

### Lighter Capital (covered in Section 1)

- **Their RBF structure is effectively a revenue-share agreement** with a 1.3x–1.5x repayment cap. See Section 1 for full details.

### General Revenue-Share Market Terms

- **Typical revenue share percentage:** 2%–8% of monthly revenue
- **Typical repayment caps:** 1.3x–3x the investment amount (lower caps = cheaper capital; higher caps = more investor-friendly)
- **Duration:** Self-liquidating — the term ends when the cap is reached, which depends on revenue growth. Faster growth = faster repayment
- **Structural advantage:** Unlike fixed-payment debt, payments shrink in slow months and grow in strong months, reducing cash flow risk
- **Key risk:** If your revenue grows very quickly, the effective annualized cost of capital can be high (you're paying back the cap faster). Conversely, if growth is slow, the cost is spread out and feels more manageable

### Revenue-Share Summary for Firmcraft

**Best fit:** Calm Company Fund SEAL for equity-like capital ($75K–$250K), Lighter Capital RBF for debt-like capital (up to 33% of ARR). Both offer capped returns with no permanent equity dilution and no exit pressure.

## Recommended Sequencing for Firmcraft

### Immediate (This Month)

1. **Apply to NVIDIA Inception** — free, no equity, stacking cloud credits and AI mentorship
2. **Apply to AWS Activate** — up to $300K in cloud credits for AI workloads
3. **Apply to Google for Startups** — up to $350K in GCP credits
4. **Apply to Microsoft Founders Hub** — limited but free; OpenAI API access has value
5. **Explore Texas FAST Partnership** — free support for SBIR grant applications

### Near-Term (1–3 Months)

6. **Prepare an NSF SBIR Project Pitch** for SkillCalibrate under the AI/workforce development topic. Budget 40–60 hours for a competitive pitch. Potential $275K+ in non-dilutive funding
7. **Engage with Calm Company Fund** — the SEAL structure is purpose-built for your profile. Reach out, share your story, understand current terms
8. **Evaluate Lighter Capital or Founderpath** — once you have 3+ months of consistent revenue data, connect your accounts and see what you qualify for

### Growth Stage (6–12 Months)

9. **SBA 7(a) loan** — once you have 1–2 years of financials and a specific growth investment in mind (hiring, marketing, product development)
10. **TinySeed application** — if SkillCalibrate emerges as the primary growth vehicle and you want accelerator community support, apply for the next cohort (September window)
11. **Capchase / larger RBF facilities** — once SkillCalibrate crosses $1M ARR

### Key Principle

These options are not mutually exclusive. The cloud credit programs (AWS, Google, NVIDIA) stack with everything else. An NSF SBIR grant can fund R&D while Lighter Capital funds go-to-market. A Calm Company Fund SEAL can provide flexible growth capital while you maintain an SBA line of credit for working capital. The optimal strategy is layered: non-dilutive first, revenue-share second, debt third, equity last (if ever).
