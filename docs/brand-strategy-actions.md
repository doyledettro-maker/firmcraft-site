## Review Notes

The report is strong. The endorsed-brand recommendation, the 80/20 concentration split, and the graduation signals framework are all well-reasoned and match Predictium's stage. A few additions and disagreements:

**Agree and adopt immediately:**
- Endorsed brand architecture ("SkillCalibrate, built by Firmcraft") — correct call
- 80/20 marketing concentration on Firmcraft — correct call
- Separate domains, no shared global nav — already in place, validates current setup
- USPTO filings for both marks — overdue, do this week
- Cross-domain analytics — prerequisite for measuring everything else
- "Built by" not "Powered by" — small words, real signal difference
- SkillCalibrate graduation signals (5 tenants, 3 external, <10% churn) — good defaults

**Agree directionally, adjust execution:**
- Brand identity shift (warm/forge → operator/credible): the direction is right but a 6-10 week design project is premature. Do the copy/messaging shift now (kill forge metaphors, use operator language). Defer the visual palette refresh until there are 3+ paying Firmcraft consulting clients — otherwise you're polishing a brand nobody has seen yet. The site we just rebuilt already uses a clean, modern aesthetic; the warm/forge identity lives more in copy than visuals at this point.
- Defensive domains: agree on skillcalibrate.ai and .io. firmcraft.com only if under $5K — don't get into a negotiation with a squatter. The .ai TLD is perfectly fine for an AI services company.

**Disagree or deprioritize:**
- Public SkillCalibrate roadmap page (quick win #9): premature with one pilot. A roadmap page with 3 items signals "early stage" to the exact buyer persona (L&D managers) who screens for maturity. Wait until there are real shipped features to list. Ship features first, roadmap page after.
- Two WorldMax case studies (quick win #6): only if WorldMax consents and the engagement is far enough along to have measurable results. Don't publish a case study on a pilot that might churn — the disqualifying signals section of the report itself flags this risk.

## Decisions Locked

These are now the operating assumptions unless explicitly revisited:

1. **Brand architecture:** Endorsed brand. "SkillCalibrate, built by Firmcraft." Predictium LLC stays invisible until Stage 4 (triggered, not scheduled).
2. **Domain strategy:** Separate domains, no shared nav, light cross-references only.
3. **Marketing concentration:** 80% Firmcraft, 20% SkillCalibrate for the next two quarters.
4. **Pricing separation:** Two distinct pricing pages. No bundling until SC exceeds 30% of monthly recurring revenue AND majority of new SC signups come from outside Firmcraft.
5. **Cross-sell direction:** Service → Software (lead with Firmcraft, reveal SC in delivery). Never lead a Firmcraft sales motion with SkillCalibrate.
6. **Content strategy:** One editorial calendar, one content engine, each piece published once on the domain where its audience lives. Cross-link, never syndicate.
